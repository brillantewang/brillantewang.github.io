TeacherPay

A visual analysis of teacher salary to student outcomes across the world

Note: The size of a country bubble represents its GDP.

Background: As a former teacher, I had heard at some point that countries that paid their teachers the highest salaries also had the best overall education systems, which implied best student achievement.

To determine if this was true, I decided to analyze data provided by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) and build TeacherPay, a simple visual tool that shows the relationship between teacher salaries and student outcomes in science, math, and reading across 35 countries. You can find more information about this project here.

Note: The size of a non-clicked country bubble correlates with its GDP.

This axis represents the percentile ranking of a country's mean score on the Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) in 2015.

For example, Poland's mean score on the science PISA was higher than 61% of all other countries.

This axis represents a country's annual teacher salary in 2015 converted to US dollars using purchasing power parity to account for cost of living.

For example, Poland's lower secondary school teachers had a mean salary equivalent to earning $25,375 in the US.
